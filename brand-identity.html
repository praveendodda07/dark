Brand Identity

“Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works.” –Steve Jobs


Brand identity plays an important role for a particular brand to grow in the industry. All the components related to a product, service, company, or person is “brand identity”. Some of the factors have name, logo, tone, tagline, typeface, and shape that create an appeal. Brand identity is a separate category from brand image.

Brand Identity is the basic idea that the consumer receives from the product, person, or thing. The brand identity will connect to product recognition. For instance, a recent street survey was done by asking people on the street to tell them the first product that comes to their mind when they hear the word, Nike? Unanimously, it was the shoes. Nike established the brand identity for their shoes.

Brand identity should be a consistent message received by its audience. If a portion of the identity is a particular shade, consistency of the colour is imperative in maintaining the product identity. The identity must match the image projected to the public.

The Purpose of Brand Identity:
The primary purpose of brand identity is setting guidelines and consistency. Whether the product is a person, image, or an object, consistency exhibits product leadership, marketing, support and operation. Consistency in identity projects the corporate culture that surrounds the product.

The key to brand identity:
The clarity of what is being offered is the essential factor in branding, whether it is a product, service, or person. Image and consistency play a vital role in branding. Branding is the big plan. It describes the expected results of a product or individual. The reputation of the product or person is essential to the branding results. When it comes to a physical item or service offered, the quality is quite often the branding technique referenced. If the quality decreases, branding results in a false image and the reputation diminished.
